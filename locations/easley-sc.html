Easley's Unique Air Quality Concerns

Easley is a growing Upstate SC community in Pickens County, conveniently located near Greenville and the foothills of the Blue Ridge Mountains. The city features a mix of historic homes from the textile era and newer construction, creating a range of air quality considerations for property owners.

Many of Easley's older homes, especially those built before 1980, may contain asbestos-containing materials and lead-based paint. These hazards often remain hidden in insulation, floor tiles, pipe wrapping, and painted surfaces until a renovation or demolition project disturbs them. Professional testing before any remodeling work is critical to protecting both workers and occupants from exposure to these harmful substances.

The Upstate's humidity and seasonal weather patterns create conditions that are highly favorable for mold growth. Crawl spaces, basements, attics, and poorly ventilated bathrooms are common trouble spots in Easley homes. Persistent moisture issues can lead to hidden mold colonies that degrade air quality and cause respiratory problems, allergic reactions, and other health concerns.

Easley also sits in a moderate radon risk area in the foothills of the Blue Ridge Mountains. The underlying geology in Pickens County can produce elevated levels of radon gas, which seeps into buildings through cracks in foundations and slab floors. Because radon is colorless and odorless, professional testing is the only way to know if your home has dangerous levels. The EPA recommends mitigation for any home with radon levels at or above 4 pCi/L.
