Lead Paint Testing: What Every Homeowner Should Know

Homes built before 1978 may contain lead-based paint, which poses serious health risks — especially for young children and pregnant women. Lead exposure can cause developmental delays, nervous system damage, and cognitive impairment. Professional testing using XRF analyzers or lab-analyzed samples is the safest way to determine if lead is present in your home.

Understanding Radon: The Silent Health Risk in Your Home

Radon is a colorless, odorless radioactive gas that seeps into homes through cracks in foundations and is the second leading cause of lung cancer in the United States. The EPA recommends taking action at levels of 4 pCi/L or higher, yet many homeowners have never tested. Professional radon testing is the only way to know if your home is at risk.

What Is Asbestos? Understanding the Risks and Why Testing Matters

Asbestos was widely used in insulation, floor tiles, roofing, and pipe wrap in buildings constructed before the 1980s. Disturbing these materials during renovation or demolition can release microscopic fibers that cause serious lung disease and mesothelioma. Testing before any renovation project is both a safety measure and a legal requirement.
